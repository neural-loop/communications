# **Open Neuromorphic (ONM) Organisational Charter**

## Preamble
Open Neuromorphic (ONM) is a global, open-source community fostering collaboration, knowledge sharing, and innovation in neuromorphic computing. Our mission is to build a thriving network of contributors by organizing talks, discussions, and hackathons that advance the state of the art.

## Purpose
ONM exists to:
*   Promote learning and experimentation in neuromorphic computing.
*   Support collaborative open-source development.
*   Serve as a bridge between academia, industry, and independent contributors.
*   Facilitate grassroots involvement across technical and non-technical roles.

## Governance Structure

ONM governance is divided into two distinct bodies to balance operational agility with long-term strategic stability:

1.  **The Leadership Council:** The elected officers responsible for day-to-day operations and execution.
2.  **The Advisory Board:** A non-operational body of stakeholders providing strategic guidance and institutional memory.

---

### I. The Leadership Council (Operational)

The Leadership Council is composed of **up to 10 Officers**. This is a working body responsible for executing the organization's mission.

**Structure**
The Council operates as a flat, democratic body. All Officers hold equal voting power.

**Functional Portfolios**
Officers on the Council align their efforts with three core **Portfolios**. While Officers may collaborate across areas, they are appointed to champion specific domains:

**1. Operations & Infrastructure Portfolio**
*   **Scope:** The "Engine." Finance, Legal, Website, GitHub, Discord permissions, and Tooling.
*   **Role Examples:** Infrastructure Lead, Treasurer, Governance Coordinator.

**2. Science & Education Portfolio**
*   **Scope:** The "Knowledge." ONR Peer Review, SNN Book, Educational Curricula, and Academic Standards.
*   **Role Examples:** Science Lead, Education Coordinator, ONR Editor.

**3. Community & Ecosystem Portfolio**
*   **Scope:** The "People." Events, Social Media, Volunteer Coordination, and Collaboration Network relationships.
*   **Role Examples:** Events Coordinator, Social Media Manager, Partner Liaison.

*Note: Multiple Officers may serve within a single Portfolio to share the workload.*

### II. The Advisory Board (Strategic)

The Advisory Board is composed of **Founding Advisors**, **Former Officers**, and **Key Community Stakeholders**.

**Role & Function**
*   **Guidance:** Advisors provide high-level input on strategy, partnerships, and ecosystem trends.
*   **No Operational Burden:** Advisors do **not** hold specific operational responsibilities. They are not expected to manage daily tasks.
*   **Continuity:** In the event of a total Leadership Council vacancy, the Advisory Board serves as the **Fail-Safe**, temporarily assuming stewardship of assets until new elections can be held.

## Council Decision-Making Process

To manage a distributed leadership body effectively while maintaining quality control, the Leadership Council employs a **Tiered Authority** model:

#### **1. Operational Autonomy (Execution)**
Officers have **autonomy** to execute standard tasks within their assigned Portfolio without seeking full Council approval.
*   *Examples:* Hosting a scheduled event, fixing a bug on the website, posting to social media, replying to routine emails.

#### **2. Process & Policy Adjustments (Peer Review)**
Decisions that alter established workflows, guidelines, or operational policies require the **sign-off (seconding)** of at least one other Officer.
*   *Examples:* Changing the pull request review criteria, altering the event scheduling template, updating the contributor guide, or modifying Discord role permissions.
*   *Mechanism:* A simple confirmation (e.g., a thumbs-up or "looks good") from another Officer is sufficient to proceed. This ensures a sanity check and alignment across portfolios.

#### **3. Strategic Consensus (Majority Vote)**
Major decisions involving the organization's direction require a **majority vote (50% + 1)** of the full Leadership Council. The following triggers **always** require a vote:
*   **Governance:** Amendments to the Charter or Code of Conduct.
*   **Finance:** Financial commitments exceeding a set limit (e.g., $500).
*   **Partnerships:** Entering into formal partnerships or MOUs with external organizations.
*   **Endorsements:** Official ONM endorsement or branding of external projects (e.g., books, libraries) to ensure alignment with open-source standards and prevent conflicts of interest.
*   **Appointments:** The appointment of new Officers to fill vacancies.

## Membership

Membership in Open Neuromorphic (ONM) is open to all individuals who support its mission and values.

#### **Membership Admission**
Prospective members must be **nominated by an existing member** or may **self-nominate** by submitting a brief expression of interest. The **Leadership Council** facilitates an **informal vote** where a **majority vote determines who is invited as members**.

#### **Active Membership Requirements**
To remain in good standing, members must:
*   Attend at least one GM within a 2-year period; or
*   Submit a written contribution to the Annual Report at least once every 2 years.

## Elections and Terms

**The Leadership Council** is refreshed annually at the General Meeting (GM).
*   **Nominations:** Fellows nominate candidates for specific Operational Portfolios based on their skillset.
*   **Voting:** Active Fellows vote to affirm the Council roster.
*   **Term:** Officers serve a 1-year term and may be re-appointed indefinitely.

**The Advisory Board** serves indefinite terms, subject to **active engagement**.
*   **Maintenance:** To retain a seat on the Board, Advisors must meet the standard activity requirements (attending 1 GM or contributing to the Annual Report every 2 years).
*   **Transition:** Advisors who fail to meet these requirements are transitioned to **Alumni status** and vacate their voting seat on the Board until they re-engage.

## General Meeting (GM)

A GM is held at least once per year.
*   **Agenda:** The Council presents the "State of the Community" and the coming year's Roadmap.
*   **Action:** The Fellowship votes to ratify the Roadmap and elect the new Council.

## Amendments to the Charter

Any member may propose a charter amendment by notifying the Leadership Council at least one month prior to a GM.

#### **Approval**
*   **At GM:** Requires quorum and 2/3 majority vote.
*   **Online Ballot:** Requires ≥50% member participation and 2/3 majority to pass.

Approved amendments take effect the day after ratification.

## Code of Conduct

The Leadership Council shares joint responsibility for enforcing the ONM Code of Conduct. Disputes involving Council members may be escalated to the Advisory Board for mediation.
